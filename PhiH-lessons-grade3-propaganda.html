Propaganda

3rd Secondary - Philosophy

General Introduction

Propaganda is a prominent social and political phenomenon in contemporary societies, playing a crucial role in shaping public opinion and directing individual and collective behaviors. Propaganda relies on a set of psychological, linguistic, and technical mechanisms to influence individuals and make them accept specific ideas or positions.

Propaganda techniques have evolved with the development of media and communication, making them an effective tool in the hands of governments, parties, and various organizations to influence masses and achieve political, economic, or social objectives.

Problematic

Problematic Questions

  • What is propaganda and what is the difference with advertising?
  • How does propaganda influence minds and behaviors?
  • What are the mechanisms used by propaganda to influence individuals?
  • Can propaganda be considered a form of symbolic violence?
  • How can an individual resist the influence of propaganda?

Text Analysis

Text 1 / Author: Hannah Arendt / Title: Hannah Arendt's Position on Advertising

Thesis

Advertising relies on science in promoting its product and is called violence.

Content

It is certain that the advertising pages of any newspaper give examples of this "scientific" character that allows a producer to prove through facts and figures and with the help of a "research service" that his soap is "the best in the world".

It is no less certain to say that there is a kind of violence in the flights of fancy of advertising experts. A mad dream of monopoly hides behind their assertion that women who do not use this particular type of soap remain pimpled and spinsters for life, a dream that makes the producer of this "only soap that prevents pimples" able one day to deprive all women who do not use this type of soap of marriage.

In the field of advertising as in the field of propaganda, science is only the product of the substitution of force.

Analysis

Advertising in Hannah Arendt's view is "a kind of violence" because it oscillates between two things: enticement and intimidation.

A. Enticement

Enticing the public to a product to the point of annoyance and disturbance, such as finding advertising for the same product on radio, television, newspapers, and the internet. This advertising is a form of violence in the author's view because you find yourself when you move around besieged by advertisements from all sides, you see it when you ride transportation and in stations, and you hear it when you walk in the street, and all this happens against your will.

B. Intimidation

And as advertising seeks to entice the acquisition of a product, it simultaneously seeks to scare the consumer from leaving that product or going to another. An example of this is that the company that produces soap claims that its soap is the only one that prevents pimples, and woe to the women who do not buy it, for they will remain pimpled for life. Advertising in this form uses moral terrorism and spreads frightening but fundamentally false information.

Advertising has its effect on the recipient and spares no one. The one who is interested in the advertised product either starts searching for it or feels frustrated by his inability to purchase it. And if he is not interested in the product, he considers the advertising as a provocation.

Critique

The direction that advertising exercises on the individual has an impact on him, this is certain, but describing it as "a practice of violence" exaggerates matters, because the concept of violence implies the meaning of domination using actual force. And this is what we do not find in advertising. The recipient's position on advertising is generally limited between enjoyment, response, mockery, or disgust, and in general, he does not seek to get rid of it as one gets rid of a whip on his back.

The reality is that not all advertising misleads the citizen. There are useful products that match the mentioned specifications, and the solution is not to eliminate advertising because there are people who cannot know about the product except through advertising, but the solution lies in exposing any advertising that misleads and presents a trivial product as good or a harmful one as beneficial.

Text 2 / Author: Weizmann / Title: The Power of Advertising and Propaganda

Thesis of the text

The author in the text shows the unity and intersection between advertising and propaganda despite their different goals.

Problematic

Where do the common points between advertising and propaganda appear despite their different fields? And how do we understand their shared influence on individuals and the standardization of their choices and minds?

Analysis

Difference between propaganda and advertising
  • Political propaganda: is a set of psychological, linguistic, and technical mechanisms used by political systems and parties to influence public opinion to support a particular system.
  • Advertising: also uses the same means to influence individuals' opinions and behaviors to promote a particular commodity and encourage its consumption over others.
Mechanisms of influence

Advertising hides the power relations embedded within its mechanisms, as the image appears honest and neutral at first, but in reality, it is not. The process of preparation, composition, broadcasting, and commentary aims to influence the individual in his political choices (propaganda field) and consumer desires (advertising field), which contributes to confusing the individual and depriving him of his freedom.

According to "Erich Fromm", both propaganda and advertising use a psychological mechanism to achieve their goals:

  • Arousing instincts (through sexual desire)
  • "Continuous repetition"

Advertising and propaganda do not address human reason but rather target his desires and emotions, thus linking the product with desire and hope for escape from despair. Therefore, we notice the intensive presence of the image of women in advertising spots and the excessive exploitation of her body. The association between commercial goods and instincts is supported through the "hammer" method, which manifests itself in the continuous repetition of advertising spots, thus embedding in the individual's subconscious everything that advertising wanted to convey and directing his choices.

Insights of the text

  • Attention to the fact that propaganda and advertising images are not simple and spontaneous but seek to influence consciousness in a hidden way.
  • Avoiding passive acceptance of the image and striving to analyze and criticize it.
  • Propaganda and advertising deal with humans as beings of desires and fears, not as rational beings capable of choosing for themselves.
  • Awareness that deprivation of freedom does not occur only through force but also through hidden, quiet, and gentle ways through which the individual believes he is choosing completely freely.

Propaganda and Science

Science is usually viewed as the culture that liberated man from religious and mythical culture and was the basis for effective technology that met man's needs and facilitated the realization of his desires in several fields. And it seems that the field where there has been enormous and even frightening progress is the field of media and communication.

Thanks to media and communication, it was possible to exchange information, messages, and sciences very quickly and between different continents, thus enabling man to benefit from the experience of other peoples and therefore develop his life.

But are the services provided by media and communication all positive, can't they be used to serve ignoble, ideological and capitalist purposes?

Conclusion

Propaganda constitutes a complex phenomenon in contemporary societies, representing an effective tool for influencing public opinion and directing behaviors. It relies on sophisticated psychological and social mechanisms that target emotions and desires more than reason and logic.

The danger of propaganda lies in its ability to shape individuals' consciousness and direct their choices without them realizing it, making it a form of symbolic violence exercised on minds. Therefore, it is essential to develop individuals' critical awareness to distinguish propaganda mechanisms and resist its influence.

Ultimately, knowledge and critical awareness remain the most powerful weapons to face the influence of propaganda and advertising, and preserve the autonomy of thought and freedom of choice.
